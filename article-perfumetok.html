How FragranceTok Became the Most Powerful Sales Channel in Perfume

March 2026

Sol de Janeiro was a niche body care brand with €26 million in sales in 2022. By 2024, after going viral on TikTok, it had become Sephora's top-selling brand of the year with $780 million in global sales — a 30x increase in two years.

Beginning in 2019, fragrance enthusiasts started posting short videos reviewing perfumes and ranking their favorite scents. Within just a few years, leading fragrance influencers grew from a few hundred thousand followers to millions, establishing a new category of online creator.

As these creators gained audiences, the broader PerfumeTok community expanded rapidly. #PerfumeTok has grown into a multibillion-view ecosystem. This surge reflected a shift in how consumers discover fragrance. Unlike traditional perfume marketing built around department stores and magazine ads, PerfumeTok relied on storytelling, aesthetics, and personality-driven recommendations.

The impact on the fragrance industry has been substantial. During the same period that PerfumeTok exploded, the global fragrance market experienced strong growth. Global fragrance sales increased from roughly $48 billion in 2019 to an estimated $78 billion by 2026. While multiple factors contributed to this growth, social media has played an increasingly central role in shaping consumer demand.

PerfumeTok succeeded by solving the unique challenge of selling a sensory product online. To combat the constraint of conveying smell in the digital realm, creators developed descriptive narratives such as describing scents as being akin to "a rich hotel lobby," "a vanilla dessert," or "the clean girl aesthetic." These stories made fragrance both entertaining, intriguing, and shareable.

Today, PerfumeTok represents more than a trend. It has reshaped how fragrances are marketed, discovered, and purchased, demonstrating the power of social media to transform even the most traditional consumer industries.
